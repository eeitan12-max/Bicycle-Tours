About This Tour

Tuscany is arguably the most beautiful cycling destination on earth. The famous strade bianche — white gravel roads — wind through the rolling hills of Chianti between vineyards, olive groves, and cypress-lined farm tracks that feel lifted straight from a Renaissance painting. This 8-day journey from Florence south to Pienza takes you through the heart of it all, at a pace that leaves plenty of time to stop at a roadside cantina and try the local Brunello.

The terrain is hilly — this is not a flat tour — but the climbs are short and manageable, rewarded by views that take your breath away more than the gradient ever will. Each night you stay in a different hilltop town or converted stone farmhouse (agriturismo), eating food grown within sight of your table. This is la dolce vita on two wheels.

Day-by-Day Itinerary

Day 1

Arrival in Florence

Arrive in Florence and check into your hotel near the Piazza della Repubblica. Afternoon bike fitting and orientation ride around the historic centre. Welcome dinner with a view of the Arno. Distance: 0 km.

Day 2

Florence → Greve in Chianti

Leave the city and immediately climb into the Chianti hills. Pass through vineyards and olive groves to Greve — the unofficial capital of Chianti Classico wine country. Evening wine tasting at a local enoteca. Distance: 45 km | Climb: 900 m.

Day 3

Greve → Siena via Radda in Chianti

The strade bianche section — gravel roads through the heart of wine country, past medieval towers and stone farmhouses. Arrive in magnificent Siena and explore the extraordinary Piazza del Campo. Distance: 52 km | Climb: 800 m.

Day 4

Rest Day in Siena

A full day in one of Italy's most beautiful medieval cities. Visit the Duomo, climb the Torre del Mangia, and explore the Contrade (neighbourhood) museums. Optional cooking class in the afternoon. Distance: 0 km.

Day 5

Siena → Montalcino

Head south through the Crete Senesi — a moonscape of rounded clay hills with solitary farmhouses on every ridge. Arrive in Montalcino, hilltop home of Brunello, Italy's most celebrated red wine. Cellar visit included. Distance: 55 km | Climb: 700 m.

Day 6

Montalcino → Bagno Vignoni → Pienza

Descend to the thermal spa village of Bagno Vignoni with its unique central hot spring piazza, then climb to Pienza — the perfect Renaissance town designed by Pope Pius II and a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Distance: 42 km | Climb: 600 m.

Day 7

Val d'Orcia Panorama Loop

The iconic Tuscan view — cypress trees aligned against golden hills, a solitary farmhouse, and not a sound except birdsong and your tyres on the gravel. This circular route covers the most photographed landscapes of the entire Val d'Orcia. Farewell dinner at the agriturismo. Distance: 38 km | Climb: 550 m.

Day 8

Departure from Pienza

After a final Tuscan breakfast of pecorino cheese and local honey, the support van transfers the group to Chiusi train station (20 min) for connections to Florence, Rome, and beyond. Distance: 0 km.
