About This Tour

The Loire Valley is the jewel of French cycling. Declared a UNESCO World Heritage Site, this region weaves together Renaissance châteaux, sun-drenched Chenin Blanc and Muscadet vineyards, and quiet riverside paths where the only sound is the breeze through poplar trees. This 7-day guided tour follows the EuroVelo 6 — one of Europe's finest cycling routes — from the city of Tours downstream to the historic port city of Angers.

Every day brings a new castle silhouette on the horizon. You'll visit Château d'Amboise where Leonardo da Vinci spent his final years, explore the extraordinary Château de Chambord with its double-helix staircase, and taste your way through villages famous for their sparkling Crémant de Loire. Accommodation is in hand-picked 3-star hotels and charming maisons d'hôtes in the heart of each town.

Day-by-Day Itinerary

Day 1

Arrival in Tours

Meet your guide at Tours train station. Check into your hotel in the medieval old town, then join a welcome dinner at a local brasserie with introductions and route briefing. Distance: 0 km.

Day 2

Tours → Amboise

Depart along the south bank of the Loire on quiet cycling paths through vineyards. Visit the Château d'Amboise perched above the river and pay respects at the tomb of Leonardo da Vinci in the royal chapel. Distance: 28 km.

Day 3

Amboise → Blois via Chambord

A highlight day: detour south to the extraordinary Château de Chambord, Francis I's hunting lodge set in a vast forest. Then return north to follow the river to Blois with its magnificent royal château. Distance: 52 km.

Day 4

Blois → Chaumont → Onzain

Cross to the north bank and pedal through lush river islands. Stop at the elegant Château de Chaumont-sur-Loire, famous for its international garden festival. Overnight in a riverside hotel. Distance: 42 km.

Day 5

Onzain → Saumur

A longer day through the Anjou wine country. Pass cave dwellings carved into tuffeau cliffs and arrive in Saumur, home of France's finest sparkling wine. Evening cellar tour and tasting included. Distance: 68 km.

Day 6

Saumur → Angers

The final cycling day follows the river downstream through the Muscadet vineyards. Arrive in Angers, dominated by its massive 13th-century fortress, and celebrate with a farewell dinner in the city. Distance: 47 km.

Day 7

Departure from Angers

After breakfast, your guide transfers you to Angers train station. Direct trains connect to Paris in 1h 30min. Au revoir! Distance: 0 km.
