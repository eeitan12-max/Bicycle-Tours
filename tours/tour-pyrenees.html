About This Tour

This is bucket-list cycling. The Pyrenees Crossing takes you from the Atlantic surf beaches of Biarritz across the spine of Europe to finish at a tapas bar on Barcelona's waterfront — 14 days, 1,100 kilometres, and some of the most legendary mountain passes in professional cycling. You'll climb the Tourmalet, the Aubisque, the Puymorens, and Puerto de la Bonaigua — names that echo through Tour de France history.

This tour is designed for fit, experienced cyclists who are comfortable with long days in the saddle and significant elevation gain. Our support van shadows the group throughout, carrying water, snacks, spare parts, and riders who've had enough for the day. Every evening you descend to warm showers, mountain-village restaurants, and the camaraderie of fellow riders around the dinner table. This is hard. It is also completely unforgettable.

Day-by-Day Itinerary

Day 1

Arrival in Biarritz

Arrive in the elegant Basque surf resort. Bike fitting, route briefing, and welcome dinner of Basque pintxos on the Grande Plage promenade. Distance: 0 km.

Day 2

Biarritz → Saint-Jean-Pied-de-Port

Head inland through Basque farmland, past bright-shuttered farmhouses and Pelota courts. Saint-Jean-Pied-de-Port is the traditional starting point of the Camino de Santiago pilgrimage. Distance: 65 km | Climb: 1,200 m.

Day 3

Col d'Aubisque

The first major col of the trip: 16 km of climbing at an average 7.2% gradient. Breathtaking views over the Pyrenees from the summit at 1,709 m. A long exhilarating descent into Laruns awaits. Distance: 72 km | Climb: 1,800 m.

Day 4

Laruns → Luz-Saint-Sauveur

Traverse the Ossau valley through the Pyrénées Atlantiques department. Rolling terrain today with a rewarding finish in the pretty spa town of Luz-Saint-Sauveur. Distance: 78 km | Climb: 900 m.

Day 5

Col du Tourmalet

The most famous climb in the Tour de France. 17.1 km at 7.4% average from Luz-Saint-Sauveur to the summit at 2,115 m. The views are worth every metre of suffering. Descent to Bagnères-de-Bigorre. Distance: 68 km | Climb: 1,900 m.

Day 6

Recovery Day — Bagnères-de-Bigorre

A well-earned easier day. Optional visit to Lourdes, or spa treatments at the thermal baths. Legs get a partial rest with a short 30 km loop through the foothills. Distance: 30 km | Climb: 400 m.

Day 7

Bagnères → Foix via Col de Port

Cross into Ariège department and ride the Col de Port. The medieval fortress-city of Foix with its three-towered château presides over the valley below. Distance: 92 km | Climb: 1,400 m.

Day 8

Foix → Andorra la Vella

Climb the Col de Puymorens (1,920 m) — the gateway to the Principality of Andorra. Descend into duty-free Andorra la Vella for a unique overnight in Europe's highest capital city. Distance: 85 km | Climb: 1,600 m.

Day 9

Andorra → La Seu d'Urgell (Spain)

Cross into Catalonia. The descent from Andorra into the Valira valley is spectacular. La Seu d'Urgell is the last major Pyrenean town before the mountains gradually relent. Distance: 40 km | Climb: 200 m.

Day 10

La Seu d'Urgell → Sort

Through the Pallars Sobirà wilderness — wild rivers, pine forests, and barely a car. Sort is famous as Europe's whitewater kayaking capital. Distance: 70 km | Climb: 800 m.

Day 11

Sort → Vielha via Puerto de la Bonaigua

The last great col: Bonaigua at 2,072 m. Vielha is the capital of the Val d'Aran, a unique Gascon-speaking enclave within Catalonia. Spectacular mountain scenery all day. Distance: 80 km | Climb: 1,700 m.

Day 12

Vielha → Lleida

The mountains begin to give way. Descend through increasingly Mediterranean landscapes of olive groves and terracotta-roofed villages to the ancient city of Lleida on the Segre river. Distance: 110 km | Climb: 400 m.

Day 13

Lleida → Barcelona

The final day: flat Catalan farmland, then vineyards, then the city swallows you up. Ride through Sarrià and down to the waterfront. Arrive at the sea. Farewell dinner at a seafood restaurant on the barceloneta. Distance: 165 km (support van option available).

Day 14

Barcelona — Departure

Breakfast, goodbyes, and the return to reality. Barcelona El Prat airport is 30 minutes by metro. Distance: 0 km.
