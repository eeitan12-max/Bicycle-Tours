About This Tour

The Danube Cycle Path (Donauradweg) is consistently voted Europe's most popular long-distance cycling route — and once you're on it, you'll understand why. This 10-day journey follows the mighty river from the three-river confluence at Passau, Bavaria, all the way to the imperial splendour of Vienna. The route traces car-free riverside paths through a living history book: Roman fortresses, Romanesque monasteries, medieval market towns, and Baroque palaces line the banks at every bend.

The terrain is gentle and almost entirely flat, making this tour accessible to cyclists of all fitness levels. The biggest challenge is deciding which apple strudel to order. Evenings are spent in characterful guesthouses and 3-star hotels where local hosts welcome you with home-cooked Austrian cuisine and cold Viennese lager.

Day-by-Day Itinerary

Day 1

Arrival in Passau

Arrive in the beautiful three-river city of Passau where the Inn, Ilz and Danube converge. Welcome meeting, bike fitting, and dinner at a riverfront brewery. Distance: 0 km.

Day 2

Passau → Engelhartszell

Cross into Austria immediately and follow the Danube through the dramatic Schlögener Schlinge gorge, a sweeping river meander surrounded by forested hills. Visit Austria's only Trappist monastery. Distance: 44 km.

Day 3

Engelhartszell → Linz

Roll into Linz, Upper Austria's vibrant capital and European City of Culture. Afternoon free to explore the Ars Electronica Centre or the old town's colourful baroque square. Distance: 58 km.

Day 4

Linz → Grein

Leave the city behind and enter the Strudengau, a stretch of the Danube dotted with medieval castles perched on rocky outcrops. Lunch in the perfectly preserved baroque village of Grein. Distance: 62 km.

Day 5

Grein → Melk

Pass through the Nibelungengau (Nibelung country of legend) to reach the crown jewel: Melk Abbey, a breathtaking Benedictine monastery rising 60 metres above the river. Guided tour included. Distance: 55 km.

Day 6

Melk → Krems (Wachau Valley)

The UNESCO-listed Wachau Valley — the most beautiful stretch of the entire Danube. Pedal through apricot orchards, terraced Grüner Veltliner vineyards, and ruined castle towers. Wine tasting in Dürnstein village. Distance: 40 km.

Day 7

Rest Day in Krems

Free day in Krems. Options include cycling the Kamptal wine valley, visiting the Kunsthalle Krems art museum, or simply relaxing in a riverside Heuriger (wine tavern) with new-vintage white wine. Distance: optional.

Day 8

Krems → Tulln

Leave the valley behind and enter the wide Tullnerfeld plain. Stop at Tulln, birthplace of painter Egon Schiele, before continuing along the riverbank with Vienna's skyline beginning to shimmer ahead. Distance: 68 km.

Day 9

Tulln → Vienna

The triumphant final ride into the imperial capital. Follow dedicated cycle paths into the city and arrive at the Ringstrasse for a celebratory group photo. Farewell dinner in a Viennese restaurant with schnitzel and Sacher torte. Distance: 52 km.

Day 10

Vienna — Departure

Breakfast and farewells. Vienna airport is 20 minutes by train from the city centre. Or stay on and explore — the city deserves at least another day. Distance: 0 km.
