About This Tour

The Dolomites are the most dramatic mountain landscape in Europe — sheer pink limestone towers rising thousands of metres above green meadows, WWI trenches carved into cliffsides, and passes with names that make any cyclist's heart race: Passo Gardena, Passo Sella, Passo Falzarego. This 12-day tour descends from Innsbruck through the Brenner Pass into the South Tyrol, loops through the UNESCO-listed Dolomites, and finishes with the most cinematic arrival in cycling: a gondola ride in Venice.

South Tyrol is a cultural crossroads where Italian passion meets Austrian precision — the cuisine reflects this perfectly, with spaghetti and schnitzel sharing menus, washed down with local Lagrein red wine. The riding is challenging, but the support van is always nearby, and e-bike options are available for every stage. You don't need to be a professional cyclist to do this tour — you need to love mountains and be willing to work for the views.

Day-by-Day Itinerary

Day 1

Arrival in Innsbruck

Welcome to the Tyrolean capital, dramatically surrounded by mountains. Bike fitting, welcome briefing, and dinner of Tiroler Gröstl and local Schnapps in a traditional Gasthaus. Distance: 0 km.

Day 2

Innsbruck → Brenner Pass → Vipiteno

Cross the main Alpine divide at the historic Brenner Pass (1,370 m), the ancient trade route between Northern and Southern Europe. Descend into Italy and the bilingual South Tyrol. Distance: 58 km | Climb: 700 m.

Day 3

Vipiteno → Bolzano

Follow the Eisack river valley south, with the dramatic Dolomite peaks emerging ahead. Arrive in Bolzano, South Tyrol's vibrant capital and home of Ötzi the Iceman at the South Tyrol Museum of Archaeology. Distance: 65 km | Climb: 400 m.

Day 4

Bolzano → Val Gardena (Ortisei)

The Dolomites begin in earnest. Climb into the Val Gardena — the heart of the Ladin cultural region and one of Europe's premier ski destinations, transformed in summer into a cycling paradise. Distance: 52 km | Climb: 1,200 m.

Day 5

Sellaronda — The Great Dolomite Loop

The centrepiece of the tour: four passes in one day — Passo Sella (2,240 m), Passo Pordoi (2,239 m), Passo Campolongo, and Passo Gardena (2,121 m). The famous Sellaronda circuit around the Sella Massif. Extraordinary. Distance: 60 km | Climb: 2,200 m.

Day 6

Rest Day in Val Gardena

Your legs deserve it. Optional easy ride on the Alpe di Siusi (Seiser Alm) — Europe's largest high-altitude alpine meadow, accessible by cable car. Afternoon spa and mountain views. Distance: optional 20–30 km.

Day 7

Val Gardena → Cortina d'Ampezzo via Falzarego

Cross into the Veneto region via the Passo Falzarego (2,105 m) with its extraordinary WWI fortifications tunnelled into the cliff face. Descend to Cortina d'Ampezzo — the glamorous queen of the Dolomites. Distance: 55 km | Climb: 900 m.

Day 8

Cortina → Belluno via Tre Cime

Detour to the Tre Cime di Lavaredo — three vertical spires of rock that are the symbol of the Dolomites, appearing on postcards worldwide. Then descend through larch forests to Belluno. Distance: 68 km | Climb: 600 m.

Day 9

Belluno → Vittorio Veneto → Treviso

The mountains recede and the Veneto plain opens up. Pass through Vittorio Veneto, site of the decisive WWI battle, and arrive in Treviso — the elegant walled city that gave the world tiramisu and prosecco. Distance: 88 km | Climb: 300 m.

Day 10

Treviso → Venice (Mestre)

A relaxed flat day across the Veneto plain, arriving at the edge of the Venetian lagoon. Check into your hotel in Mestre for your final two nights. Evening vaporetto to Venice for a celebratory dinner in Cannaregio. Distance: 45 km | Climb: 20 m.

Day 11

Venice Free Day

A full day to explore La Serenissima. Visit the Doge's Palace, cross the Rialto, get lost in the sestieri. Evening farewell dinner in a traditional osteria with cicchetti and Aperol Spritz. Distance: 0 km.

Day 12

Departure from Venice

Final breakfast and farewells. Venice Marco Polo Airport is 15 minutes from Mestre by bus. Alternatively, catch a train to Rome, Milan, or Florence. Distance: 0 km.
